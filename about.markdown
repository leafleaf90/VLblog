<h1 id="about-heading">About</h1>

<p>I'm Viktor Lövgren, a software developer writing about code, tools, digital sanity, and the ongoing quest for fewer tabs open.</p>

<p>This site is where I keep notes from building things: Jekyll, Nuxt, Vue, Firebase, small automation projects, newsletter experiments, and the occasional attempt to make technology feel less like a drawer full of mystery cables.</p>

<p>The writing is mostly practical. Sometimes it is a tutorial. Sometimes it is a field note from debugging something that should have taken fifteen minutes, which is a genre of software literature with a rich and terrible history.</p>

<p>I also write about productivity, digital minimalism, reading, mindfulness, and the general problem of using technology without letting it quietly move into the driver's seat.</p>

<p>Projects mentioned here include Vegan Monkey, Em Dash Patrol, 15 Pages, Blue Tiki, and Stop Football Plastic. Some are active, some are retired, and some are reminders that "small side project" is usually a warning label.</p>

<p>If you want new posts by email, use the newsletter signup on the homepage or below posts. It is manual, not an automated firehose. Civilization depends on small boundaries.</p>

<p>You can reach me at <a href="mailto:mail@viktorlovgren.com">mail@viktorlovgren.com</a>, find me on <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/viktorlovgren/">LinkedIn</a>, occasionally on <a href="https://x.com/viktor_lovgren">X</a>, or browse my reading notes on <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/viktorlovgren">Goodreads</a>.</p>
